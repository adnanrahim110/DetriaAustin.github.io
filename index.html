About The Book

Detria Austin Everson’s “Serving Is a Superpower” is an absolute stunner that speaks to the life-changing presence of serving others. She digs deep into the profound journey of finding one’s purpose through service by sharing her experiences and reflections. Austin has attached so much weight to serving with words, actions, and influence; she has represented what it is to put others before one in growing and fulfilling purpose. Austin also brings to the forefront the rough times in service and how it can affect both the server and the served in a positive way. Helping others to true success—unlocking the full potential of serving—is an exciting guide informed by practical advice and inspiring stories, all present in “Serving Is A Superpower”.

What Keeps You Turn The Pages?

The engaging storytelling, the relatable experiences shared, and the powerful message about the transformative power of serving others are all elements that keep you inspired to turn the page and continue reading. The personal anecdotes and insights offered by Detria Austin Everson create a sense of connection and inspiration, making you eager to learn more about how serving can be a superpower in our lives. Additionally, the promise of practical advice and inspiring stories further piques my interest and motivates me to keep exploring the book’s content.

Author

Detria Austin Everson

President and CEO of the John and Lillian Miles Lewis Foundation. The Foundation, conceived by Congressman John Lewis, carries forward the Lewis’s legacy of purposeful living and civic engagement to build the “beloved community”; while inspiring and supporting “Good Trouble.” Detria’s exceptional leadership and unwavering commitment to civil and human rights align with the Foundation’s mission to increase civic participation, build social and intellectual capital for youth, and protect democracy. She believes in the power of community and collective action to create lasting change, leading programs that strengthen democracy and amplify the voices of rising generations. Previously serving as the Founding Executive Director at the Martin Luther King Sr. Community Resources Collaborative, Inc. in Atlanta, Ms. Everson has been at the forefront of initiatives aimed at breaking generational poverty. Her tenure as Chief Operating Officer at the Neighborhood Assistance Corporation of America (NACA), the nation’s largest nonprofit mortgage and U.S. Dept. of HUD-Certified counseling agency, saw her orchestrating nationwide operations, securing significant corporate funding, and facilitating multi-city homeownership tours, benefiting thousands of underserved communities. During her tenure at the Augusta University Medical Center (Formerly MCG Health, Inc), Detria’s role as Assistant Vice President for Administrative Services underscored her commitment to healthcare innovation and patient well-being. Her focus on culturally competent care and diverse functions within the premier teaching hospital and her ability to manage vital funding resources showcased her multidimensional expertise in leading impactful initiatives. Ms. Everson’s impressive track record of leadership, strategic planning, and effective execution make her a highly respected and accomplished professional in her field. Her academic credentials include a Bachelor of Science in Business from Spelman College, Atlanta, Georgia, and a Master’s in Health Services Administration from the University of Arkansas at Little Rock. An active member of Antioch Baptist Church North, the National Alumnae Association of Spelman College and board member of the Winthrop Rockefeller Foundation, Detria is committed to fostering a brighter, more inclusive future for all.
